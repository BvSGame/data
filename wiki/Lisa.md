## Dr. Elizabeth Ausbuloff

During her time in [2024](2024.md), Lisa became a professor in a [secondary technical university](Secondary%20technical%20university.md), much to the shock of [Saylor](Saylor.md) and [Rick](Rick.md).

Lisa has made a fake identity, naming herself Dr. Elizabeth Ausbuloff (also, jokingly, Dr. Liz). Essentially, “Ausbuloff” is an anagram for her catchphrase, “Fabulous,” with an extra F, which she explains as a “flavor letter which stands for ‘frantically,’ making it ‘frantically fabulous.’” Saylor finds it incredibly cheeky, especially when she first deciphers the anagram as “Doctor Fabulous.”

### Classic outfit

- Lisa’s appearance can be described as “a mix of a mid-20th-century schoolgirl and an astronomy professor.”
- Lisa has jet-black hair with a slight reddish tint. She usually wears her hair in a ponytail tied with a colored bow (normally green).
- Lisa prefers this combination of clothing:
	- Velvet jacket—color may vary;
	- White shirt;
	- Colored neck bow—may match the hair bow or not;
	- Pleated skirt;
	- Black stockings;
	- Heeled shoes.
- Lisa’s eye color is red.
- Height: 5’11 / 180 cm.

## Trivia

- In the story *[Fleeing the Urban Chaos](Fleeing%20the%20Urban%20Chaos%20(story).md)*, Lisa claims to have a favorite anagram for her name, which is Alis. Saylor makes two references: to *Alice in Wonderland* and to the *BvS* character [Alice](Alice.md) (who didn’t exist as a concept when *Fleeing* was written). However, you can discover various other references:
	- [A.L.I.C.E.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_Linguistic_Internet_Computer_Entity) (Artificial Linguistic Internet Computer Entity) is a 1995 chatbot.
	- [Alice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alice_(programming_language)) is a programming language.
	- [Alice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alice_(Microsoft)) is a project from Microsoft related to artificial intelligence.
	- [Alice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alice_AI) (now known as Alice AI) is a Russian chatbot and virtual assistant from Yandex.
	- With some stretch, you may also notice a reference to IRIS, a virtual assistant in the movie [*App*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/App_(film)).
