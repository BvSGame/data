## What is Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers?

<div align="center">

![](https://media.bvsga.me/text_assets/bvsmedley_whatisbvs.png)</div>

“Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers” (or BvS for short) is a multimedia indie franchise. First of all, it’s intended to be a sci-fi-themed series of games of various genres—primarily it’s tower defense, but it’s mixed with visual novel and side-scrolling RPG. Beyond that, BvS can also be expanded to books, comics, audio dramas, and standalone music albums.

If we go with the analogies, see tower defense aspect as Plants vs. Zombies, narrative scope as Doctor Who, and musical score as a little bit Genshin Impact. The game is planned to have cutscenes primarily in a motion comic format, every cutscene is skippable and can be even shortened (via game settings)—with some exceptions.

Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers is in active development since 2023, when a reboot of the project happened, overhauling everything: lore, music direction, actual introduction of characters (original BvS lacked characters completely). Before 2023, there were many concepts since early 2010s, and the earliest piece of BvS content exists since 2014.

Story was retconned and rewritten. Now, as I (liledix⁴) learn screenwriting and storytelling, there are about 60,000 words written in total, from all drafts combined, and the number keeps growing rapidly. There are at least 14 established characters, each with their own memorable quirks and compelling background.

The starting premise of the game series is this: “sleepwalkers” are androids, service robots that help optimizing the workflow in almost every profession. A batch of these robots were donated to the town by a mysterious engineer, about whom nothing is known at first (but he’s later revealed as Darkrock). Those androids have a glitch: at night, when they’re intended to preserve power and recharge, they’re “waking up” for about 3 minutes and walk aimlessly—that’s why they’re called “sleepwalkers.” Saylor, who is the protagonist and tech student, was called for an investigation of this strange behavior. However, as she was completing this task, she got into trouble. Who knew that this trouble would lead… to the time travel and cosmic conflict.

Currently I, liledix⁴, am working on this game alone, however my friends provide a lot of support. One day I’m planning to commission artists and programmers to make this game real, not just a set of concepts.

Games and code are entirely open-source (AGPL) and freely moddable. Assets like music are freely published on platforms like Archive.org (with Creative Commons license). Screenplays are accessible as rendered PDF and raw text files (Fountain format, specifically). Even every used font is strictly non-proprietary, with an open-source license.

Starting from 2023, development stages are explicitly broken into what has been called WIPs, now they’re called Milestones. The naming choice is quite specific: while WIP (Work In Progress) is too formal, Milestone neatly ties to the narrative itself, pointing at the ongoing, continuously unfolding adventure in space and time. 2023 reboot split Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers into two eras:

- **Legacy** (2014–2022), when liledix⁴, formerly 3do, did the whole thing completely alone.
- And now-called **Milestone 1** (2023–2024), with help of the most important co-contributor, ZaBlazzingZephyrus, and some other friends.

Current **Milestone 1.5**, with liledix⁴ working alone again (temporarily), is started mid-2024 with numerous revisions. Since then, there are attempts to move to the next stage, **Milestone 2**, with the plans to actually work on the first proper playable demo to mark the transition to **Milestone 3**.

## About Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers Medley

<div align="center">

![](https://media.bvsga.me/text_assets/bvsmedley_aboutmedley.png)</div>

Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers Medley (BvS Medley, or just Medley) is the largest, breakthrough musical event in the history of Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers franchise so far. 29 tracks representing the soundtrack of the games, composed by liledix⁴, with more orchestral and cinematic approach, kickstarting a new era of BvS production.

Originally, creation of BvS Medley was started in February 2026, with the private notes about it existing since September 2025. At first, proper release was scheduled to February 22, 2026, because visually the date looked like “``2.22``,” and the numerology always was important in BvS (after all, we’re dealing with sci-fi genre where nothing is a coincidence). However, that release didn’t happen. Attempt to postpone it to March 1, to match the 2nd anniversary of BvS YouTube channel, is failed as well. Instead, Part 1 of Medley was released with three tracks (Medley Intro snippet, Future on Fire, and Lisa). It has been followed by 6 more releases of Medley Parts, until it’s been decided to set a final deadline that can’t be moved: May 29, 2026, because of 3 supporting factors:

- **Track amount.** Since February 2026, it’s been established that Medley would have 29 tracks. Since then, the plans weren’t changed.
- **Creation date of BvS YouTube channel.** It’s been created February 29, 2024.
- **Birthday factor.** liledix⁴, the lead contributor, is turning 29 the next day, May 30, 2026.

So, it’s the date that will never happen again. Medley *will* happen even with the unresolved production issues. But, it’s planned to polish Medley later for the Deluxe Edition, specifically inviting other composers and artists to push the initial vision even further beyond. Deluxe Edition of BvS Medley is scheduled to June 26, 2026 (numerology at work again, it’s 2s, 6s, and 26s now).

## Disclaimer! No Generative AI!

<div align="center">

![](https://media.bvsga.me/text_assets/bvsmedley_disclaimer.png)</div>

It’s deeply ironic that I’m about to throw some bombs at the actual clankers here. 😄

Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers as a multimedia project (not just games, it could be comics or audio dramas as well) needs artworks so, so much! I, liledix⁴, am not an artist, I can only put images together neatly, but that’s “packaging,” not art. That’s why I’d really welcome the participation of artists who can help move the project forward, even if just by a tiniest step. Please-please-please. 🙏

So, I exactly know why AI slop hurts the project in many ways. And not just project. By introducing any smallest element of AI generation in this project, it signals people: “look, it is fine to use AI in a creative medium”—no, it’s not! AI usage is signaling: replacing artists with the cold machines is good, contributing to the environmental impact is good, keeping destroying a remaining decent and humane part of internet is good.

Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers as an indie project DOES NOT tolerate that!

[![](https://images.squarespace-cdn.com/content/v1/58b55acc9de4bbb8c782ad6a/1739544182081-JQSLZ30CE1GABR2H4PCX/NoGenAI_Seal_Yellow_NoShadow.png?format=400w)](https://www.polygon-treehouse.com/no-gen-ai-seal)

I’m already struggling with finding decent stock images that aren’t an AI slop. A few times I even failed to spot those as an AI slop. And that’s bad, I ditch that as soon as I find out. But wherever I can create something myself, and it’s music, writing, web apps, websites—I DO NOT need AI even for automating or simplifying routine! Every smallest decision here is a CONSIDERED effort! It’s an expression of experience, it could be messy, but at the end of the day, it’s human.

In case of text. To be honest, I always considered the “em dash” argument flawed and blind to nuances. I know people normally don’t use those in the casual conversations. But it doesn’t mean those are necessarily difficult to put into the text if you *intend* to, to make it look clean, professional, and easier to read. Mobile keyboards support em dash: just hold your finger on the hyphen, and there you have it. In Windows, it’s not difficult to remember the ``Alt+0151`` combination, not to mention Emoji Panel (``Win+.``) and Character Map. Some programs and apps even allow to put two or three hyphens in a row to get en dash and em dash, Obsidian even has a plugin for that (called Smart Typography). Hell, you can just go google and copy-paste! I’m really geeking hard over punctuation, so give me a pass on that. 😄

Despite the open-source nature of the project, I cannot tolerate AI training off of Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers content. AI can’t possibly comply with the AGPL license, training without explicit consent of the creators is an actual violation of the license terms. However, realistically, I also know AI-bros are an unstoppable force, they will not step away easily, they won’t concede just because I asked nicely.
