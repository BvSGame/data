# Draft 5

[[May 3, 2026]]

2024. As usual, @S’s rental. *Stability!* Indoors.

@L, having her “professor” look, sits on the sofa, immovable. Absolutely neutral face expression. But eyes open. Not blinking at all. The dictionary definition of “frozen.”

@S, dressed in her classic denim overalls, comes and notices that.

S: Lisa?

@S looks into @L’s eyes. @L doesn’t respond at all. She’s absolutely still. Zen master still. @S snaps her fingers in front of @L’s face.

S: Hello? Is your ping okay? I think you’re losing some traffic packets. With terrifying consistency.

@L doesn’t respond. @S grabs @L’s shoulders and tries to shake her lightly.

S: What’s wrong with you? We talked half an hour ago! You can’t just die!

@S pauses and removes her hands from @L’s shoulders.

S: Okay. I’m going on a mission alone. Enjoy your solitude. If I die, I won’t leave a note. Just keep in mind.

@S slowly walks away.

L: Stop.

@S stops.

L: (sighs in relief) Operation complete.
S: What?
L: (looks at @S) I said. Operation complete. Yay!
S: (comes to @L) What was that?
L: What do you mean?
S: You looked dead.
L: I can’t die. I’m a software in a hardware shell.
S: No, that was an expression. You were sitting here completely immobile, you were reacting to nothing. What were you doing the last few minutes?
L: Oh? Ah, of course. I was coding.
S: Coding?
L: Yes. What you described to me is my usual behavior during an intensely focused coding and compiling session.
S: What were you coding?
L: That would be telling.
S: Never mind.
L: Actually, during the compilation process, I also patched myself. Well, y’know, self-patching is my hobby.
S: What have you updated?
L: “Fabulous” is my favorite word now.
S: Hm. You know, it fits you. Yeah, it does… It works. I like it. Keep that.

@S is about to walk away, but she gets interrupted.

L: Uh, Saylor. Do you have a moment?
S: Yeah?
L: Allow me to demonstrate a new accessory.
S: This doesn’t sound right…

From her inner pocket of a velvet jacket, @L pulls out a fob watch.

L: The Fab Watch!
S: It’s *fob* watch.
L: *Fab*. You, watch. At first, it looks like an ordinary watch. You still see hours, minutes and seconds. But, this watch has more hands. There are multiple smaller watch faces. First indicates a day, month and year of the current century. Second indicates a century and millennia, but it’s limited to 100,000 years. From 50,000 BC to 50,000 AD. Zero year is a central point.
S: Uh. It’s impressive but… pointless? It’s mechanical!
L: Nuh-uh! Digital!
S: What?
L: It has mechanical appearance, and ultimately it’s a camouflage. But inside, there’s a tiny motherboard that does calculations based on many real-world variables. You don’t set the time manually.
S: Totally useless. You’ve grabbed a toy you can’t even fine-tune.
L: Wrong again. You’re on a roll with your classic undervaluing. Shake this watch hard enough, and it’ll receive a command for automatic recalibration. A calculation will be triggered that will scan the surroundings and assume local time.
S: Then what is this button for and what is the third mini-watch-face for?
L: Stopwatch.
S: Ah…
L: Short press, ticking starts and pauses. Long press, it resets.
S: Okay.
L: Button has some other patterns too, like self-destruction, but I’m not spilling the beans casually.
S: No, Lisa, you’re wrong.
L: What?
S: You think I don’t know how your brain works? Plant some idea insistently enough, and you won’t resist the analytical thought process and immediate output.
L: Not only you underestimate this piece of hardware, but also you underestimate me. I have a strong blacklist. It’s encrypted even for me, I don’t know what it contains.
S: Therefore, you don’t know if you’re allowed to tell me the self-destruct pattern or not.
L: I simply don’t want to.
S: Okay. Look, I’m teasing you. I’m actually impressed with this thing. It’s…
L: Stellar?
S: Fabulous.
L: It is *Fab*.
S: No.

@S walks away, around the room, getting ready for something.

L: Where now?
S: The mission. I feel hungry for the adrenaline rush.
L: Every day, you’re coming back from the university drained like a squeezed lemon with a human face, but sometimes you’re too merciless to your own body.
S: A mess of chemicals, aren’t I?
L: You’re impossible. Illogical. Irrational.
S: Thank you.

@S pauses.

S: Uh… You still haven’t said why you need that… Fab Watch thing for. Maybe it has some practical use that went over my head.
L: I just enjoy how it ticks.
S: Okay. Each to their own.

@S keeps going.
