# Draft 10 - Garage Door Opens

[[April 17, 2026]]

@S’s temporary house. One week earlier in 2024. This is a slight rewind. That “zap” didn’t happen yet in the lives of @R and @C.

We see @R sitting at the table in the kitchen. Fiddling with some complicated device, unscrewing something or putting something in place. Everyone else is gone. Again.

Suddenly, @S breaks the silence, reaching out via @R's smart watch.

S (VO): Rick?
R: Uh. Yeah, Saylor? Finally, I hear you now, it’s been a while. Where have you gone?
S (VO): It’s been only half a day, man. Don’t exaggerate your feelings.
R: I just care, y’know. I need to know what’s happening. Any fun in 47th century?
S (VO): Dull place. We’ve lost Alice.
R: What?!
S (VO): Calm down. Don’t worry. She’s fine. You know her, show her a basic rock from another time, she would be excited. If only I had one hundredth of her commitment.
R: You’re still making me worry.
S (VO): Are you trying to say we had to take a vibe killer like you to some random adventure?
R: Now you’re making me a damsel in the castle.
S (VO): Anyway. There’s something urgent. Sorry, gotta go. I’ll talk to you later! Nice to know you’re alive.

Transmission cuts out.

R: Pfft. Alive. Boredom would kill me earlier… Oh well.

Somewhere in the distance, from outside, a familiar “flash” can be heard. @R pauses for a second but keeps fiddling. The door opens, @C enters and notices @R.

C: (calmer than ever) Hi.
R: Oh, hi! Uh, pardon me, there’s a… thing to do.
C: Do you have a few minutes?
R: (pauses, looks at Charlotte) Something happened?
C: Uh… No. Er, yes. Well. I don’t… I’m not sure.
R: (noticing @C’s mind is cloudy, puts device and spare parts aside) Something really happened. (nods to the chair)

@C slowly sits down.

R: You know the deal. We’re friends. If something makes you uneasy, just feel free to share it. I won’t judge you or anything. But please, don’t keep this worried look.
C: I can’t help myself, Rick.
R: Well. What could one of the strongest girls in the team be conflicted about?
C: Anything may bypass the shield and hit the core… I saw your past.
R: (slowly nodding) Okay… Carry on.
C: 2018. Hadn’t you noticed how Saylor behaved? When you were around.
R: Charley, there are some doors that must remain shut. I prefer not to go in there ever again.
C: You’re way too ironic here.
R: What do you mean?
C: At some point, Saylor was doing her usual garage activity considerably slower.
R: Everyone needs a break from time to time. She was growing tired.
C: You’re not as oblivious as you pretend.
R: What?
C: You know what was happening. She was trying to fix you.
R: It’s her duty of care. It’s normal for her.
C: No, Rick, listen. A teenage mind. It went off the rails. She was behaving clumsily. As if she tried to hide something that keeps showing up.
R: I remember that. Tired, I’m saying.
C: In her eyes, you would read something beyond tired. She herself started feeling broken.
R: Ridiculous implication.
C: You don’t remember the moment when she took action? When you were about to leave the garage, and Saylor used her remote to close down the garage door?
R: I said. I don’t want to go in there, in these memories.
C: You know what happened then.
R: Well. Yes.
C: What is it?
R: Charley. You’re interrogating.
C: No, I just want to hear it spoken from your mouth.
R: Really?
C: (putting hand on @R’s shoulder) You have nowhere to go. Speak it.
R: You’re impossible…
C: Well?
R: Sigh… (blushing, looking away) We… we kissed.
C: Say it again?
R: (looking straight) It happened! Yes! So what?
C: Hah! Surrogate siblings! For goodness sakes!!
R: Look. You’re being unfair to Saylor. She might have feelings. We agreed to let it go. It doesn’t matter. That was an attempt to cause total emotional breakdown, and it’s been a failure. We don’t recall it since.
C: And here you’re lying.
R: Huh?
C: Saylor told me all of this. I just had to go see for myself.
R: Are you two plotting a new round of breaking me down?
C: Ha-ha. No. Mind you, I regret my teenage self hasn’t been around at that time. Saylor wouldn’t need to go to these lengths ever.
R: Don’t make it weird.
C: Oh I’m trouble. Such a trouble. But hey, nothing stops me from kidnapping myself and bringing to you. I wonder what happens.
R: Don’t. Ever. Do. That.
C: I’m tempted. Sorry.
R: Why have you even decided to start this conversation?
C: Ah… Ehh… He-he. Nothing special, really. I’m just… being funny, y’know! It’s my, my business. Being a time-space clown. This… doesn’t land right, yeah?…
R: No. Not at all. (proceeds to keep fiddling with the device) You’re acting out of your character.
C: (blushing) O-okay. I think this thingamajig is a better conversationalist for now… I better get g--
R: --No. Can you stay here for a while? The silence kills me.
C: Well, if you say so. Hm, look at that A2 capacitor, that’s your primary problem.
R: Oh. Yeah. You’re right. How did you notice?
C: Rick. You’re the oblivious one here. You still wonder?
R: Anyways. This transistor does concern me as well…
C: There’s nothing wrong with it. You have an unstable transformation of the current flowing through this part of the circuit.
R: Ri-i-ight… I believe there must be some fitting replacement, hang on…
