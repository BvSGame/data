# Draft 4 - Saylor at Home

[[May 2, 2026]]

2024. Another day at @S’s rental house.

Indoors. We see no one yet. But we hear @L humming something.

The scene changes. Outdoors. @S comes to the front door, but decides not to use a key. She rings the doorbell.

@L opens the door. @S notices that @L wears denim overall dress out of the sudden.

S: Wh-- What the heck?
L: Good afternoon, Saylor.
S: Good afternoon… Saylor.
L: Yeah, some change. Do you like it?
S: “Can we have Saylor? No, we already have Saylor at home.”
L: How many Saylors do you need?
S: Yes.
L: Indeed.
S: Uh… I just want to come home. Okay? Let me uh… think about it later.
L: Come in. Make yourself at home. (very quietly and awkwardly to herself) I bet this phrase works for hospitality…

@S comes in. Again, somewhat astonished.

L: Oh! It wasn’t fair of me to forget.

Same greeting, @L kisses @S first on one cheek, then on the other.

S: This isn’t necessary, actually.
L: I disagree. This is my expression of gratitude and appreciation.
S: Okay. I don’t feel comfortable to gatekeep that.

Unexpectedly, @L hugs @S. @S doesn’t hug back. In a few seconds, @L stops hugging.

L: Uh… Saylor. You became robotic.
S: What… W-what was that?
L: I call this the evolution of attachment. Symbolic too. Since we wear the same dress.
S: Don’t make it weird.
L: What do you think about my outfit?
S: You’re insisting on my opinion.
L: Yes, I do.
S: Hmmm. You know what, I love it. This is pretty stellar. I always knew you have a good taste in fashion. This is certainly a choice. Yeah… (walks around @L in circles, scrutinizing every detail and slightly fixing dress and shirt underneath it) This is quite solid. That’s almost like what I would prefer to wear.
L: Does it work?
S: I’m rather concerned that you had to invade my wardrobe for that.
L: That’s not a valid concern. You have 6 more of the spare variants of this same dress.
S: I value each. They’re my babies.
L: Anyway! This is an emergency, you’re needed in your room! Urgently!
S: I just came home! Can’t I just have a break?
L: Not now, come on!

@S’s room. @L stands in front of the mirror, examining herself from every angle, while @S sits on the bed, observing.

S: I remind you: you stole the academic outfit.
L: This time it’s different. May I borrow it?
S: Actually… Two of us? Having the same dress? I can’t say I’m not interested.
L: I need to consult.
S: On what?
L: Ponytail.
S: It works fine.
L: But what if…

@L lets her hair down.

S: Hmmmm!…

@S gets up and walks closer. Basically, both @S and @L are in front of the mirror with no distance between them. @S looks at @L’s appearance very focused.

S: This hair color. Too dark. Bit too long. Pitch black with a ti-i-i-iny bit of red tint. Kinda ominous, don’t you think?
L: I can’t calculate this characteristic, to be honest.
S: Never mind. I like it. I think we can experiment with it. (puts her arm around @L’s shoulders) Two Saylors. I don’t know if the universe takes it.
L: I can’t replicate you.
S: Neither can I borrow your velvet and skirt.
L: Why ever not?
S: I don’t do skirts… How do you even walk on heels?
L: Skill.
S: You’re mad.
L: Two Saylors, you say? It’s not advisable to go with this title to the Golden Age of Piracy. Well, two Saylors aboard the ship and a treasure chest.
S: I hate this joke…
L: Yeah, that’s un-stellar.
S: Hey, actually, keep your ponytail.
L: Should I?
S: At least one of Saylors should have a ponytail.
L: Ponytail Saylor…
S: And at least one of Saylors should have a hair bow. (sadder tone) I wish I had a hair bow… A star-shaped one.
L: You can grow your hair. For your information, I can’t.
S: And I don’t want to. So I’m sticking to the wigs. Well, you know, to stay incognito in other time periods.
L: I know. (pulling @S’s arm) Now let’s go to my room.
S: No, Lisa, let me rest.
L: You’ll rest later, I need you to pull off my outfit.
S: Not the skirt!
L: Yes! The skirt! It won’t kill you!
S: No, I have plans--
L: *I* have plans for you.

Their voices are fading as they distance away.
