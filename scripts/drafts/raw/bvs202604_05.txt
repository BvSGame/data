# Draft 5 - Sunshine

[[April 8, 2026]]

2024, SAYLOR’s temporary house. There’s yet another character sitting at the table in the kitchen, playing the Game of Fifteen. @C enters (she has a key just like many other characters SAYLOR trust).

C: Oh, I was looking for you, Alice.
A: O-o-oh! Fancy skirts with the wrist calculator here! Hiya!! I actually wanted to ask why you're dressed like that, almost always, no matter where we go.
C: Because I like it! Simple as that.
A: Must not be your first dress.
C: Does it matter? What matters is an impression.
A: Yeah, yeah, alright.
C: No, but I do adapt to other time periods.
A: What, by changing shades of colors?
C: No. You haven't seen me in a spacesuit yet. I did that, once.
A: Yeah, anyways. Where are the others?
C: Taking a walk. Somewhere. I don't track them, y'know. You’re from 3000s, aren’t you?
A: That's right! But y'know, Saylor says I'm, uh, what was it... Time-agnostic. Something like that.
C: Ah, same thing. Damn, I’ve got to revisit it… 3000s. It’s in my bucket list. If, well, I won’t accidentally kick the bucket before that. Or else I would have nothing to write for, ha-ha.
A: (confused, pointing at parasol) Uh. This. Why do you have this? Is it about to rain tonight?
C: Wh-- Uh, no? This is parasol, sunshine. It’s supposed to protect from the sun.

@A bursts in laughter.

C: I see. There’s some flaw in logic clever Alice just found.
A: This is dumb. First, you won’t believe it, sunlight is everywhere and it will reach you one way or another.
C: Ahem. We’re standing in a room. See any sunlight touching us?
A: There are still windows!
C: Right… You’re saying “even if it rains, you’re still wet”… Go on.
A: Second, this umbrella won't hide you from the heat surrounding your skin!
C: Really? As you see, no part of my skin is exposed but my face.
A: Ah, you would rather be a cheese bun.
C: Pardon?
A: Layered but melting inside.
C: It doesn't matter that much.
A: Heh, no sense of precaution, okay. And third, have you tried to get closer to the sun? It’s a very, extremely hot sphere! Your umbrella would just catch a flame.
C: It’s not designed for this kind of protection.
A: Then it’s good as a funky stick!
C: Funky st- funky stick?!
A: Does it shoot at least?
C: Well, heheh, how to… demonstrate…
A: Oh you can’t, can you?
C: Are you ch-challenging me?!
A: Well, do it then!!
C: Fine! You just watch me!!
A: I am watching. Nothing happening--
C: --Just yet! Now look! Snap!!

@C points the parasol at the fridge and snaps the parasol open. Bolts fly from the parasol and strike the fridge, knocking it out. @C gasps, her palm covers the mouth, she feels very uneasy.

A: O-okay. That’s…

@A touches the canopy and gets lightly statically shocked.

A: That’s something.
C: (squeaky, with uneasy shoulders) I hope Saylor won’t get mad.

And after some interrupting noise that feels like “the machine coughs,” the fridge works again.

C: Phew.
A: Can you show it again?
C: No. You’ve just drained its power.
A: Oh you can't drain the refrigerator. It's still operational. You can’t kill it. (approaching the fridge) There’s something locked in the freezer section so stubbornly that we wonder if there’s a magic sandwich or something stupid. But somehow this whole shebang is powering up Saylor’s rusty 1980s SUV for time hops. A bolt strike is nothing to it.
C: No, I mean my parasol. You’ve drained it.
A: What a useless stick. So you need to charge it like a mobile phone. From what, lightnings?
C: Finally, the smartest observation you’ve said so far.
A: Don’t get all happy Victorian. After all, you’re the one with a stick. Damn, at least it didn’t turn you into a pile of ash. Y’know, the grounding rod thing.
C: Yeesh, that’s terrible…
A: Shocking experience, isn’t it? I wouldn’t pass x-ray like that.
C: That’s… not how x-ray works?
A: I know. I’m kidding.
C: (light laugh without smile) Yeah. The kid is kidding.
A: Well. Enough comedy. Rick is gone and I'm awfully bored. I need a playmate. Right now. I wanna see what you're made of.
C: No, no, Alice, I'm not in a mood for games-
A: Come on, come on, come on, I need you.

The voices are now distancing as @A pulls @C's arm while they walk to the front door.

C: No, stop it--
A: --Well, your parasol is discharged, you have nowhere to go. Well, I know where WE can go, and I can't go alone.
C: That's a pity, isn't it?
A: Not really, you're here.
C: Whoever almighty out there in the sky and beyond, help me.
A: You'll pray later.

And they're leaving.
