# Draft 2 - The Internet Librarian

[[May 2, 2026]]

## Part 1

It’s 2024. @S and @L are just taking a walk. @S is known for her immeasurable love for bubble-gum flavored snacks and drinks. Now, they step out of their time machine, which is 1980s SUV powered by a vintage fridge, freezer section of which is permanently locked, it certainly contains something of an immense power and nobody knows what it is.

S: I'm here to kick arse and chew bubble gum... and I'm all out of gum.
L: Um… Saylor. This sounds inappropriate. Kindly limit this language.
S: Yeah. I know. Bit of an understatement.
L: No.
S: Don’t be a vibe killer. We have a mission ahead. (gently grabs @L’s head from behind and turns slightly) Ahead is there, my lady.
L: Just move on, shall we?

They start walking.

S: (offers some gum) Gum?
L: First, I may have taste sensors, but I don’t comprehend the habit of chewing whatever this is, consumable rubber. Second, Saylor, you’re the dictionary definition of inconsistency.
S: Thanks.
L: Okay, I’m kidding. I know what gum is. Give me some.
S: A robot chewing a gum. Life didn’t prepare me for that.
L: Well. I’m only trying to be human.
S: Another thing I wasn’t prepared for. You’re in for a streak today, aren’t you?
L: (puts her arm around @S’s shoulders) Oh, Saylor. I have lots of surprises in store for ya.

## Part 2

2024. @S and @L get back to @S’s temporary/rental house.

They’re having a rest. @L reads some old newspaper, somewhere from 1990s, aloud. She wears spectacles. Meanwhile, @S browses something on her tablet.

L: Okay. That’s an L.
S: Lisa. Don’t.
L: What?
S: Robot built in 2345. Dressed like a schoolgirl from mid-20th century. Reading 1990s newspaper. Commenting with 21st-century slang. I don’t even know if we’re hitting a temporal paradox already. But my brain is bombed.
L: So? Is it cringey?
S: Really don’t.
L: Cringey then. No cap.
S: Stop it, please.
L: A little vibe check won’t hurt you, Sayley.
S: I feel sick…
L: Oh, look! (turns the newspaper to @S and points at some picture) Skibidi!!
S: I’m out for a minute.

@S walks away.

L: Okie-dokie. Don’t be a slow-mo.

Three minutes later. @L removes the spectacles and puts the newspaper away, prepared for @S’s return. And she returns.

L: Oh. Saylor. Welcome back.
S: Yeah.
L: How do you feel?
S: Nice to see you.
L: Not better then. Look, I’ve just processed something.
S: Oh no…
L: No, but look. The phrase: “bad jokes.” Why does it sound close to “dad jokes”?
S: In your case, I’m afraid those are synonymous.
L: I’ve observed something more.
S: I can’t wait to know.
L: Try to mirror “bad” in “bad jokes,” and you’ll get--
S: (grumpy) No…
L: Yes.
S: No, don’t--

@L dabs.

S: Eat my soul… Lisa, you’ve got a virus…
L: Am I going to die?!
S: No. *I* am going to die. Thank you.
L: What an anticlimax after ejection.
S: Just stop. Can you stop? Do you have a stop button? Or at least a self-destruct button? Something for you to shut up?
L: Well. There are six-seven, I mean, sixty seven ways of how I can be stopped, but all of them are tricky.
S: Did you just meme again?
L: (innocently) No?
S: You did.
L: No.
S: I can sense it.
L: Even if I did, it’s from your future.
S: So you did.
L: Shut up.
S: Okay. I remembered.
L: 67 is… (trying to literally shut her mouth with a palm) from late 2025-- Forget it!
S: (smirking) An internet librarian slips.
L: I mean, I could have 2 more ways of being stopped but they’re now obsolete because… they’re too effective-- (her palm covers her mouth again) Making it a total of-- Just shut up!!
S: (light laughs) Stellar. Now I begin to enjoy this hell. Your brain has a disadvantage, it can’t stop being analytical!
L: (with the mouth shut, higher pitched) I hate it, I hate it, I hate it…
