10 minutes later. They never left the kitchen.

R: No. You’re here.
C: Well, I would appreciate it if you didn’t classify me as a thing.
R: Oops! It didn’t occur to me. Sorry.

@C laughs cutely.

C: No problem. I’m learning to live with that. Your mind is torn.
R: You’re very perceptive. It’s never silent in my head…
C: Maybe it’s a defense mechanism of your brain. It makes itself noisy to hide you from the… actual heavy silence you’ve got.
R: Yes… But hey. 13 years of living with that. I think I have a degree by now.

Both are laughing.

C: Look… Don’t mind me being awkward today, alright? That’s… not my normal behavior. You were right to notice I was out of character. Because I was.
R: Charley. Don’t beat yourself up. You’re fine.
C: I want to finish.
R: Yeah, yeah.
C: I… I really, truly enjoy your company. And seriously, I like you. As a person, you’re great, I haven’t seen anyone like you.
R: Oh…
C: But it’s… against my nature.
R: I understand.
C: It’s a curse. I’m not allowed to settle down. I’m always running. So what am I supposed to do? How am I supposed to deal with this? I had many people I was deeply interested in, and it does hurt me that I’m not allowed to stop, to give them time they deserve.
R: Charley…

She’s crying.

R: You don’t need to stop. You shouldn’t be running alone either.
C: I can’t picture it.
R: Well. Maybe we’ll figure out something for you.
C: Promise?
R: Promise of the seasoned broken guy. Welcome to the club.
C: I don’t feel honored somehow.

R: Oh so we’re measuring me now.

R: You forgot Lisa and Alice.

R: Well. I hate it here.
C: Now you owe me a story. I’m all ears. Tell me everything.
R: And you’re not leaving until you hear it all?
C: Not moving an inch.
R: You really are a trouble, I hate you…
C: Thank you.
R: So you know about Saylor and Marie then.
C: Yes.

C: She isn’t even a real person.

R: No, you don’t get it. She is too real for that. Have you heard her inclination for getting a physical body?

R: I’ll be in trouble…
C: Why?
R: She has a… weird behavior directed at me specifically. I’m taking it as a joke, but I’m afraid she means it.

R: Just leave me on some deserted planet, please…
C: And Alice?
R: She’s interesting. She’s incapable of forming any kind of bond at all. That’s her thing. She’s living in the now, always. Something explodes, she cheers and moves on. Romance? She doesn’t even have a concept of it. Doesn’t mean I’m not interested in her, because she carries no burden and she doesn’t care about the burden of others, which is a good thing. A healthy distraction.
C: Well, mate… You’re a mess.
R: I guess that’s what I won.
C: No. You’re wrong.
