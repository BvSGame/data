# Draft 12, Part 1/2 - Historical Intrusion

[[April 19, 2026]]

J: (talking to @T) So. Chernobyl nuclear disaster. The worst we know. Happened in 1986. A nuclear power plant exploded. By the way, the most interesting part is, we still don’t quite know how many people died from that. It’s pretty up in the air.
S: (echoing from short distance) James. That pun is terrible.
J: You see, I love this subject. This is a golden mine for any researcher, big and small.
T: (laughs) Dude. You're a walking Wikipedia.
J: I know. Everyone told me that.
T: Shame. I expected to be original.
J: (stops walking, confused) Wait. You’re a gunslinger girl from 1860. How do you even know about Wikipedia? It won’t exist until 2001.
T: Sigh… Time traveler.
J: Ah… Ah yes. Naturally.
T: Mind you, Saylor was born 2 years after.
S: So. The Reader of the Books. I have a question for you.
J: I know. What time it is. Saylor, you’re such a tease.
S: Well, I wanted to see your unspoiled reaction.
J: Not like hiding the date panel would stop me from knowing.
S: Well? What would a history student say?
J: Uh… Um… Alright. You’ve got me.
S: (very quietly celebrating her win) Yes!
J: This time period is like frozen in time. No matter if it’s 90s, 2000s, or 2024, or 2050s, this place will never change.
S: It’s 1992. April 7th. Tuesday.
J: Ah. Fresh after the dissolution of the Soviet Union. But that…

Pointing at the bunch of sleepwalkers seemingly collecting radioactive materials from the plants, far away.

J: That is a violation of what we know about history.
S: Or maybe that’s an actual history and you’ve heard disinformation for your entire life.
J: Bad attempt at gaslighting. It only proves that you yourself know this shouldn’t be happening. We have to be unsafe here. In peril. Melting like wax figures.
S: Seeing the sleepwalkers doing it was enough already to understand someone is toying with history.
J: Your… Darkrock fella?
S: Exactly.
J: Who is he anyway?
S: The one who sends the sleepwalkers to me. Not long before I discovered a time machine sitting in the garage of the temporary house I moved in while I was called for the investigation of glitching behavior of service robots. My bet is: he’s after something that is inside my fridge.
J: That fridge… powers up your time machine.
S: Yes.
J: Mad.
S: I know. But I roll with it.
J: Double mad.
S: Stop pretending like you wouldn’t be curious if you were me.
J: Anyway. So let me get this straight. You have something of enormous power, and you don’t know what it is, and this thing belongs to a guy who wants to get it back.
S: I have no idea who it belongs to.
J: So you’ve decided it belongs to you.
S: Uh. For now. Let’s say, I borrowed it. But I promise to give it back.
J: Saylor… You make my brain radiate.
S: Thanks. (proudly moves on)
