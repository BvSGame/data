# Side Note - The Calm Before the Storm

[[April 20-21, 2026]]

It’s April 20, 2026. Quiet evening in the flat. Low light. We see @L frantically typing something on a phone. Most likely, this is the 13th Draft for Bombs vs. Sleepwalkers. Even if there’s no one around, he’s mumbling the thoughts as they’re being penned.

L: And then Saylor and Jenesis meet up on the asteroid. The Jenesis’s Palace. A tea party. Yes, again. Damn, it’s really that much tea in these stories… (sips tea) Anyway. So, both Saylor and Jenesis are at the table. Saylor says: “Ha! The choices you offer. If me-enemy eats an apple, I would rather take acid.” Then Jenesis claims she saw this coming, and she eats an apple demonstratively. Seeing Saylor being unamused, Jenesis offers Saylor another apple, saying it’s malic acid and that she doesn’t tolerate self-harm. Ha, that was clever. Very clever.

A sound of zap happens in the distance, outside.

L: Uh… Is that some kind of a joke?

@L checks the window. He notices someone is waving at him. But he can’t quite figure out who it is. It’s too dark. That person moves on.

L: Uh… Interesting. I’m not expecting guests today. Uh, where was I?… Ah. Right. I think I should add a chess match. Wait, does Saylor even play chess?… Well, Science Olympiad girl, she has to. Probably.

A few minutes. Someone knocks on the front door.

L: At least no one has a key but me, hah. Coming!

@L gently opens the door. He sees…

L: Wait.

Closes the door. He’s shaking his head in utter disbelief. But he collects himself and opens the door again.

L: Sorry. What the hell?
C: So you recognize me then. My name is Charlotte.
L: Are you sure you haven’t mistaken?
C: Certainly not. I have an info.
L: W-what info?
C: Look. (shows the 1980s brick phone unfolded to a mini-computer) Lisa. She located liledix4. That’s you.
L: Weird. Okay. Come in, I guess. My neighbors are not that friendly. I don’t need them to call the police.
C: Oh. Entering a micro-palace, am I?
L: Nothing that posh.

@C enters.

C: Damn. That’s cozy.
L: Well. Living alone. I don’t need much space. Uh, tea is running out, sorry.
C: Alone, you say? I know another person who prefers to stay alone. I don’t advise it, it’s a bad experience for your mental health.
L: So, why are you here?
C: You do recognize me or don’t you?
L: But… No offense, but you’re a fictional character. I created you in my stories. You’re not supposed to exist at all.
C: Well. Seems like I do exist. Sorry for that. (checking TV) Wait. You’re just as clever as I am!
L: Never mind. I’ve connected TV as a second display to my laptop. And what I write on my phone appears on my laptop, then it appears on TV. It’s called synchronization.
C: I know what it is. You don’t have to tell me. I have a room with 15 computers synchronized in 2047. It’s my space for coding projects as a hobby.
L: That’s… not in my stories yet!
C: Oh, you won’t keep up with my life. It’s impossible. So! What have you wrote so far? Man, it’s like reading a diary. Probably I don’t even have to write one myself.
L: This time, it’s not about you.
C: Oh? And also, you lied about tea.
L: That’s my mug.
C: I don’t care. (sips and pauses) Wait. You. You are ruthless!
L: Wha-why?
C: Seriously? Bubble-gum flavored tea?
L: I like it. Problem?
C: You’re kidding! Saylor would kill for this! You must know she’s a number-one fan of everything bubble-gum flavored! And here you are. Drawing all the stellar fire onto yourself. No, man, that’s criminal. (drinks casually)
L: Anyway. So. This time, this is a story about Saylor and Jenesis meeting up in the Jenesis’s Palace. Something I already wrote months ago, but that’s not a part of my latest drafts…
C: So what do they do?
L: Mental chess match. Wait, does Saylor play chess?
C: No. She prefers golf.
L: Okay. Now I want to imagine Saylor and Jenesis playing golf.
C: See? I materialized some ideas for you. By the way, they already played it. Several times. Don’t thank me.
C: Ooh. What is that folder?
L: Don’t open it. Alright?
C: Why? I’m extremely curious. Why would you put a music folder inside a folder with the dia- I mean, scripts? Rhetorical question.
L: Whatever. Do anything you want. I guess I can’t stop you.
C: Stellar.
L: Wh-what?
C: Saylor is not here. I can say it anytime I like. (browsing the folder) O-o-oh my.
L: I’m in trouble.
C: (looking at @L, performatively) Oh. My.
L: I know.
C: That’s a soundtrack!
L: So?
C: Are you… a composer or something?
L: Well… yeah.
C: I’ve heard some of those.
L: H-how?!
C: You really think that music was non-diegetic?
L: You’re not serious…
C: Well. Not quite. Only I heard those. Maybe Saylor heard her theme somewhere or even played it on her piano. Don’t think too much about it. It may spoil your further writing. Or else you would characterize me as hearing every single note, and actual me sitting right here would be furious! Seriously, I warn you.
L: Wait. You’re not saying that every decision I make about you is changing real you. Well, if it can be put like that.
C: No. It doesn’t work like that. How do you even write those… diaries so accurately? Are you aware of what’s truly happening or what?
L: No idea? No one tells me what’s happening in your world. It’s… just my mind does something.
C: Interesting. It’s almost like you’re a prophet and you’re not even aware of it.
L: Let’s not get into these depths, okay? I’m just a guy. Writing whatever pops up in mind. It’s not that important.
C: Somehow you don’t make that impression.
L: Really. You’re overthinking it.
C: Let me be a judge of that. You’re writing about us, but you haven’t seen us, and yet it’s accurate. How does it work?
L: If only I knew. I seriously don’t know! Don’t ask me!
C: Hm. I suppose you should face Saylor and others. To explain that to them.
L: No. Don’t do that. Do you remember you’re meeting them out of order? You would spoil them their future! You would be the reason behind paradoxes! Do you need that?
C: Hm. Fair argument. I would accept that. For now. But be warned. I’m watching it carefully. Oh! Oh!! Medley! I want to see Medley! You’ve released 5 complete tracks so far, but I see… every of 29 planned tracks has solo composed. O-o-oh, Jenesis’s theme! Its alternative title is “Stellar Gaslight.” Can I hear it?
L: Sigh, be my guest…
C: Oh I would play it anyway, ha-ha.
C: Oh. That Medley doesn’t contain my theme yet.
L: Uh. Well.
C: And no theme for Rick.
L: Y-ye-e-e-eah, heh.
C: No theme for Alice? Marie? But you have themes for Toni, Saylor and Darkrock in the list. Even Lisa has her theme, already finished, for goodness sakes!
L: Look. Medley has been started and planned out in February. You were… (gesturing the quotation marks) “written” in April. Of course there would be some themes missing. It’s a pretty condensed work.
C: I see. So, would you um… “compose” me someday soon?
L: (slightly worried) I-I don’t know, heh, that’s a very unusual request, ha-ha. Or perhaps the most unusual request I ever had. It’s not every day you hear from uh, what I *thought* would be a fictional character, to compose for her. If not just anyone asking to compose for themselves.
C: Well. Considering the themes of others are that good, I believe mine would be a banger too. Damn, I really like Saylor’s theme, it’s so layered and melodic, I want something like that.
L: (slightly ashamed and unguarded) Heh, thank you. Uh, there’s something I want to ask.
C: Yes?
L: Your… umbrella.
C: What about it?
L: Is it really the same umbrella that allows you for collecting the electric energy for your time travel?
C: Yes. That aspect was mentioned by you spot on. So it’s real.
L: So. If you decide to leave… You will break the fuse box, right? I mean… Neighbors would kill me for that. I surely won’t be able to explain ridiculous electric bills.
C: Ha-ha-ha. Don’t worry. I have enough power for, well, at least 7 more travels, I would believe. Besides… Hm.
L: Yes?
C: Why do you think I’m gonna leave anytime soon?
L: Oh no.
C: We haven’t unpacked your hundreds of BvS Legacy tracks yet, and you lied about your lack of tea. And then, I really want to take a long walk across the city in the morning.
L: Uh. One… little problem.
C: What is it?
L: There’s… enough space only for me to sleep.
C: No problem. You can leave.
L: Ri-ight…
C: But keep the laptop here, okay?
L: (bows theatrically) Your wish is my command, mistress.
