# Draft 13 - Age Gap of Terror

[[April 21, 2026]]

A distant asteroid. @J’s Palace.

Time machine arrives. @S leaves it alone. No companions.

Dark corridors barely lit by flames. She approaches the large doors and pulls out a tablet from her handbag. As soon as she starts her operations, an electronic voice interrupts.

L: Saylor. I’ve calculated the margin of error, it’s 76.8% and rising, you’re behaving irrationally and impulsively. Saylor. You’re making a grave mistake, you must stop and get back. Saylor. You’re an idiot! Drop your foolish commitment!

@S is busily typing a long password on her tablet. 30 characters, 40…

S: Shush. I’ll deal with it. On my own. I don’t need your assistance. I don’t need companions. That’s between me and her. Catch some bugs and… bugger off.
L: Fine. Move to your target. But keep in mind. I object!
S: Stellar.

After finishing typing 129 characters, the interior heavy locks are making a loud clicking sound. @S pushes open the large doors and enters the hall. @J smoothly walks down the stairs. We hear slow but very characteristic clicking of the heels.

J: Ah! Saylor! Enjoyed your acid?
S: That was sweet.
J: I’m glad.
S: Your hospitality is bordering with hostility. I like this non-verbal language you speak.
J: You know me. I love keeping you unharmed.
S: You don’t have to tell me why. So, you called. What’s in your agenda this time?
J: Mainly you. I don’t even need tea. Sit down.

Both @S and @J walk to the rich table and sit down synchronously.

J: So. Shall we talk about age?
S: Age? You’re a phenomenal conversationalist indeed. I went through all the space miles to talk about weather. You can do better than that, Jen.
J: I’m 35.
S: No way…
J: Don’t I look like 35?
S: No. I would give you 26 at most.
J: Oh I remember I took special care of myself at that point... Memories. Sweet, sweet memories. Sweet like an apple.
S: Hang on a minute!
J: Ah. This familiar look. Saylor doing her math homework in her mind. So Science Olympic.
S: You... Said you’re 35.
J: Ye-e-e-es?
S: I’m 21.
J: Ye-e-e-e-es?
S: I’m from 2024.
J: Ye-e-e-e-e-es?
S: If, just for a minute, I believe you, and we assume you're me...
J: Yes?
S: You’re... You’re from...
J: Say it.

Faces of @S (getting more and more nervous) and @J (getting more and more desperate) are switching and slowly zooming in. We also see the large numbers slowly growing. 2024, 2025, 2026…

J: Say it, Saylor.

2027, 2028, 2029…

J: A final stretch.

2030, 2031, 2032…

J: The last breath.

2033, 2034, 2035…

J: Say it!

2036, 2037… The numbers disappear. Close-up on the totally unnerved @S's face, lost of every bit of confidence we know she ever had.

S: (gasps, quietly, slightly high-pitched) You’re from 2038.
J: It took some time. But you’ve reached your destination. Well done.
S: 2038. The exact time when Darkrock becomes the President of Earth.
J: Coincidental, isn’t it?
S: This is not a coincidence! I assume, that’s the point when you became his boss!
J: Oh I love you… You’re so, so clever. The puzzle is completing! See the bigger picture?
S: Who are you?

@J chuckles.

S: Seriously. Who are you?
J: I’m Jenesis.
S: Are you me or aren’t you?
J: Big question. I may be or may not be.
S: Settle down!! I must know what happens to me that I become you.
J: So you could stop me? Ha. Nice try. I’m not suicidal, thank you.
S: I don’t tolerate you. I won’t become you. I despise your methods. This won’t be my future. Never!
J: Oh, you underestimate your arrogance.
S: I’m not arrogant!
J: Even if you’re not, you will be. You won’t even notice. That would work as a catalyst. That would break you. Then, once you realize it’s too late, Jenesis will be born.
S: And I will die…
J: Nothing so dramatic. I would keep calling myself Saylor, but it doesn’t stick.
S: Don’t you even dare to try my name on yourself.
J: I have no reason to wear your name. It’s a reminder of what I’ve overcome. I’m not regressive. I’m progressive.
S: Progress can be achieved in many ways. But your way of achieving it… This is not Saylor way. And never will be.
J: Stellar!

@S furrowed her brows.

J: Oh. Right. I must drop this catchphrase. It’s out of date. I prefer… (posing with the arm, palm up) Exquisite!
